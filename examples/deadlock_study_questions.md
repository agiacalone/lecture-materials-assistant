# CECS 326 Reading Assignment: Deadlock

### Assignment Description
Answer the following questions based on your reading and *Deadlock*. Be complete with your answers. You may work on these questions with one or two other partners, but *all* students must submit the document individually in their own repositories along with each student's name documented with the submission.

1. Define Deadlock.

2. Summarize the main idea behind Coffman conditions.

3. Apply Deadlock to one of today's case studies.

4. Explain how Motivation: A Real Bug, 57 Years Later would change under a different workload or constraint.

5. Choose one tradeoff from the lecture and defend a decision.

6. Compare two approaches from the lecture and identify the stronger fit for a real system.

7. Identify the failure mode most likely to appear if the lecture's guardrails are ignored.

8. Evaluate how the lecture's design choices affect observability, performance, and correctness.

9. Build a short argument connecting the framework to an unfamiliar scenario.

10. Critique the lecture's case study and propose a better alternative.
